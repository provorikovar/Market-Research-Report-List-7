<p><p>The card-based electronic access control system market primarily comprises two types: standalone and networked access control systems. Standalone systems operate independently, controlling access to a single door or entry point, making them suitable for smaller applications. In contrast, networked systems connect multiple access points through a centralized network, enabling real-time monitoring and management across larger facilities. This scalability and integration with other security protocols make networked systems ideal for businesses with extensive access control needs.</p></p>
