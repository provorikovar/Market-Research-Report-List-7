<p><p>The gynecological cancer drugs market encompasses three primary types of treatments: chemotherapy, targeted therapy, and hormonal therapy. Chemotherapy uses cytotoxic drugs to kill rapidly dividing cancer cells, effectively controlling disease progression. Targeted therapy employs agents that specifically attack cancer cell markers, minimizing damage to healthy tissues. Hormonal therapy involves modifying hormonal levels to slow or stop cancer growth, particularly in hormone-sensitive tumors. Each treatment type plays a critical role in managing various gynecological cancers and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
