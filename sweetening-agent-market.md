<p><p>The sweetening agent market is divided into two main types: high-intensity sweetening agents and low-intensity sweetening agents. High-intensity sweeteners, such as aspartame and sucralose, provide a significantly sweeter taste with minimal quantities, often used in diet and low-calorie products. In contrast, low-intensity sweetening agents, like sucrose and fructose, offer a more natural sweetness but require larger amounts for desired levels. This division caters to varying consumer preferences for taste, health considerations, and product formulations across food and beverage industries.</p></p>
