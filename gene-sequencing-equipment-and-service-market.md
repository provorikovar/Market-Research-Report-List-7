<p><p>The gene sequencing equipment and service market is categorized into four primary types. First-generation sequencing, known for its Sanger method, provides high accuracy but is time-consuming. Second-generation sequencing, or next-generation sequencing (NGS), allows for the rapid analysis of multiple DNA sequences simultaneously, revolutionizing genomics. Third-generation sequencing uses single-molecule techniques for real-time data acquisition, offering longer reads and greater resolution. Fourth-generation sequencing continues this trend by focusing on ultra-fast, highly accurate techniques, further enhancing genomic analysis capabilities.</p></p>
