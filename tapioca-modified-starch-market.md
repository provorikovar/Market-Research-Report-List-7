<p><p>The Tapioca Modified Starch market includes three primary types: Physical Modification, Chemical Modification, and Resistant Starch. Physical Modification alters starch properties through heat, moisture, or mechanical processes, enhancing stability without chemical changes. Chemical Modification involves treating starch with reagents to improve functionality, such as thickening or gelling. Resistant Starch resists digestion, acting as dietary fiber, and is used in health-focused products. Each type caters to specific industrial needs, from food processing to pharmaceuticals, offering tailored solutions for texture, stability, and nutritional benefits.</p></p>
