<p><p>The Indwelling Urinary Catheter (IDC) market is segmented into two primary types: Indwelling Silicone Catheters and Indwelling Latex Catheters. Silicone catheters are preferred for their biocompatibility, reduced risk of irritation, and longer usage duration, making them suitable for patients requiring prolonged catheterization. In contrast, latex catheters are often more cost-effective but can cause allergic reactions in some patients. Both types serve essential roles in managing urinary retention and facilitating bladder drainage in clinical settings.</p></p>
