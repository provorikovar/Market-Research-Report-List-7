<p><p>A steam reforming catalyst is a substance used to accelerate the chemical reaction in steam reforming, a process primarily employed to produce hydrogen, syngas, and other valuable chemicals from hydrocarbons like methane. These catalysts typically consist of nickel-based materials supported on alumina or other refractory oxides, as nickel is highly effective in breaking down hydrocarbons in the presence of steam.</p><p>The Steam Reforming Catalyst Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for hydrogen in industries such as refining, ammonia production, and methanol synthesis. The global push toward clean energy and decarbonization is further boosting the market, as hydrogen is seen as a key component in achieving sustainability goals. The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13.1% during the forecast period.</p><p>Recent trends in the market include the development of advanced catalysts with improved efficiency, durability, and resistance to poisoning. There is also a growing focus on catalysts that can operate under lower temperatures and pressures, reducing energy consumption and operational costs. Additionally, the rise of green hydrogen production, which uses renewable energy sources for steam reforming, is creating new opportunities for catalyst innovation. The Asia-Pacific region, particularly China and India, is emerging as a major hub for market growth due to rapid industrialization and increasing investments in hydrogen infrastructure.</p></p>
