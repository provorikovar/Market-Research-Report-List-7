<p><p>The natural hardwood charcoal market is primarily divided into two types: hardwood lump charcoal and charcoal briquettes. Hardwood lump charcoal is made from whole pieces of hardwood that are carbonized, offering a clean, pure burn with a high heat output and minimal ash. In contrast, charcoal briquettes are manufactured from compressed charcoal dust, often with added binders and fillers, providing a more consistent shape and burn time. Both types are popular for grilling and cooking, appealing to different consumer preferences.</p></p>
