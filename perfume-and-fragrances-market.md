<p><p>The perfume and fragrance market consists of several types, each distinguished by concentration levels of aromatic compounds. Eau de Parfum (EDP) typically contains 15-20% fragrance oils, offering a longer-lasting scent. Eau de Toilette (EDT) has a lighter concentration of 5-15%, making it suitable for everyday wear. Eau Fraiche contains around 1-3% oils, providing a very light fragrance experience, ideal for hot weather. Eau de Cologne (EDC) features about 2-5% oil concentration, known for its fresh, citrusy scents, often used as a refreshing splash.</p></p>
